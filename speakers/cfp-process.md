# Call for Proposals (CFP) Process

This document outlines how ProductTank Chandigarh sources and evaluates speaker proposals.

---

## When to Open a CFP

Open a CFP when:
- Planning an event 6+ weeks out
- You don't have speakers confirmed from your existing pipeline
- You want to diversify voices and discover new talent in the community

---

## CFP Announcement Copy

Post this on LinkedIn, Meetup, and social channels:

---

**We're looking for speakers for the next ProductTank Chandigarh!**

Are you a Product Manager, Engineering Leader, Designer, Founder, or anyone working on building great products? We'd love to hear from you.

**What we're looking for:**
- Real-world stories and lessons learned (not sales pitches)
- Topics around product management, UX, engineering leadership, metrics, business modelling, or growth
- 20-minute talk or a 5-minute lightning talk

**Who should apply:**
- Any experience level — first-time speakers very welcome
- Chandigarh / Tricity based preferred; remote considered
- Startups to enterprise — all welcome

**Apply by:** [date]

Submit your proposal: [Google Form link / email]

`#ProductTankChandigarh #CFP #ProductManagement #SpeakerCall`

---

## CFP Submission Form Fields

Use a Google Form or Typeform with these fields:

1. Full name
2. Job title & company
3. Email address
4. LinkedIn profile URL
5. Talk title
6. Talk abstract (max 150 words)
7. Key takeaway for attendees (1–2 sentences)
8. Preferred format: 20-min talk / 5-min lightning talk
9. Have you spoken publicly before? (Yes / No / It will be my first time)
10. Anything else you'd like us to know?

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Score each proposal 1–5 on:

| Criterion | Weighting | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Relevance to PM / product community | 30% | Directly applicable to the audience |
| Originality / freshness of topic | 25% | Avoid repetitive "intro to agile" content |
| Real-world grounding | 25% | Stories, data, lessons > theory |
| Speaker engagement potential | 20% | Will this create a good discussion? |

## Response Templates

### Acceptance

> Hi [Name],
>
> Great news — we'd love to have you speak at ProductTank Chandigarh! Your talk on "[topic]" sounds like a fantastic fit for our community.
>
> I'll send over a speaker brief shortly with all the event details and what to expect. Please confirm your availability by replying to this email.
>
> Really looking forward to it!
>
> [Organiser Name]
> ProductTank Chandigarh

---

### Decline (politely)

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you so much for submitting a proposal to speak at ProductTank Chandigarh — we really appreciate you putting yourself forward.
>
> Unfortunately, for this particular event the slots are filled, but we've added your proposal to our speaker pipeline for future events. We'll be in touch when we think the timing is right.
>
> Thanks again, and we hope to see you at an upcoming event!
>
> [Organiser Name]
> ProductTank Chandigarh
